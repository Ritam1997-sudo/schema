What is Organization Schema Markup?

Organization schema markup is a type of structured data that provides search engines with detailed information about your business or organization. It's one of the most fundamental and important schema types for any website, as it establishes your brand identity, credibility, and basic information that search engines use to understand and display your organization in search results, knowledge panels, and other rich features.

When you implement Organization schema markup on your website, you're providing explicit signals to Google, Bing, and other search engines about who you are, what you do, where you're located, and how to contact you. This structured data helps search engines verify your legitimacy, understand your business relationships, and connect your brand across the web. Organization schema is particularly valuable for building brand authority and appearing in Google's Knowledge Graph.

The Organization schema type supports a wide range of properties including your company name, logo, contact information, social media profiles, founders, employees, awards, and much more. Our generator focuses on the most essential properties that every business should implement, but the Schema.org vocabulary offers dozens of additional properties you can add to provide even more context about your organization.
