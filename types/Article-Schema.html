üí° Advanced Article Schema Best Practices

Content Section Structure

When adding content sections, mirror your actual article structure. If your article has 5 main H2 headings with supporting content, create 5 schema sections matching those headings. This structural alignment between your visible content and schema markup is crucial for search engine understanding. Professional schema markup generation maintains perfect synchronization between markup and content.

Image Requirements

Use high-quality images at least 1200px wide for optimal display in rich results. Images should be directly relevant to the article content. Google recommends 16x9, 4x3, and 1x1 aspect ratios. Host images on your own domain using HTTPS. Multiple images can improve rich result appearances, so include relevant imagery throughout your article beyond just the featured image.

Author Page URLs

When possible, link to dedicated author pages (like yoursite.com/author/john-smith) in your Author schema. These pages should have their own Person schema markup. Connecting Article schema to Person schema creates a knowledge graph relationship that builds author authority over time. Consistent author attribution across multiple articles through proper schema markup generation strengthens individual author profiles.

Update Modified Dates

When you update an article significantly (not just fixing typos), update both the visible "Last Updated" date and the dateModified property in your Article schema. This signals to search engines that content is fresh and triggers re-evaluation. For time-sensitive content, updated dates can significantly impact rankings and visibility in news-related search features.

Consistent Publisher Info

Use identical publisher name and logo across all your article schema implementations. This consistency helps search engines build a strong organizational identity for your brand. Your publisher logo should be a square image (1x1 aspect ratio) at least 112x112 pixels, though 600x600px is recommended. Quality schema markup generation maintains strict consistency across all article implementations.

Word Count Accuracy

If including word count, ensure it's accurate. Most text editors show word counts, or use online tools. Word count signals content depth to search engines. Articles over 1000 words with comprehensive schema tend to perform better for informational queries. However, quality matters more than quantity ‚Äì don't inflate word count artificially. Accurate metadata strengthens overall schema markup generation credibility.
