Service Overview

When Is Tooth Extraction Needed?

Tooth extraction is recommended when a tooth is too damaged, infected, loose, impacted, or causing repeated pain and complications. In some cases, removal may also be needed for orthodontic planning or when wisdom teeth are creating pressure and inflammation.

At our dental clinic in Goregaon West, every extraction is planned only after proper assessment. Whenever possible, we always consider tooth-saving options first before recommending removal.

Comfort, Precision & Proper Healing

Good oral surgery is not just about removing the tooth — it’s also about careful planning, gentle technique, and smooth recovery.

Treatment Types

Types of Extraction & Surgical Care

🦷

Simple Extraction

Used when the tooth is visible and can be removed without a more complex surgical approach.

🛠️

Surgical Extraction

Recommended when the tooth is broken, difficult to access, or requires controlled surgical removal.

🧠

Wisdom Tooth Removal

Impacted or partially erupted wisdom teeth may need removal when they cause pain, swelling, or damage to nearby teeth.

🔥

Infection Control

Extraction may be necessary if a severely infected tooth cannot be restored successfully.

📐

Orthodontic Need

Sometimes selected teeth are removed to create space for planned orthodontic correction.

🩹

Healing Guidance

Aftercare instructions are an important part of treatment to support uneventful healing and reduce complications.

Common Reasons

Signs You May Need Extraction or Oral Surgery

💥

Severely Broken Tooth

If the remaining tooth structure is too weak to restore, extraction may be the safer option.

😣

Repeated Pain or Infection

Persistent infection or failed previous treatment can make tooth removal necessary.

🦷

Impacted Wisdom Tooth

Wisdom teeth can cause pain, swelling, food trapping, and pressure on nearby teeth.

🩸

Loose Tooth

Advanced gum disease or trauma may leave a tooth too loose to retain properly.

📏

Orthodontic Space Planning

Some cases require selected extractions to help straighten crowded teeth properly.

📋

Specialist Evaluation Needed

Treatment Process

Clinical Examination
