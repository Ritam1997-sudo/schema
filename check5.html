Periodontal Treatments

Gum health is essential for maintaining strong teeth, fresh breath, and long-term oral stability. Periodontal treatment focuses on identifying and managing gum disease before it leads to tooth mobility, gum recession, or bone loss.

From gum assessment and deep cleaning to supportive periodontal therapy, treatment is designed to control infection, improve gum health, and protect your smile from further damage.

Service Overview

Why Gum Treatment Is Important

Gum disease often begins quietly with bleeding, swelling, or bad breath, but if ignored it may progress deeper and affect the supporting bone around the teeth. This can eventually lead to recession, loosening of teeth, and long-term oral damage.

At our dental clinic in Goregaon West, periodontal care focuses on identifying gum problems early, reducing infection, and helping patients maintain healthier gums with the right ongoing support.

Strong Teeth Need Healthy Gums

Treating gum disease early can help prevent deeper tissue damage and protect the long-term support around your teeth.

Treatment Focus

What Periodontal Care Can Help With

🩸

Bleeding Gums

Gums that bleed while brushing or flossing may be showing early signs of inflammation.

😮

Bad Breath

Persistent bad breath can be associated with plaque buildup and gum infection.

🦷

Deep Cleaning

When buildup extends below the gum line, deeper cleaning may be required to reduce bacterial deposits.

📉

Gum Recession

Receding gums may expose root surfaces and increase sensitivity or long-term structural concerns.

⚠️

Tooth Mobility Risk

Untreated periodontal disease can affect the bone support around teeth and increase looseness over time.

🛡️

Maintenance Therapy

Follow-up periodontal maintenance is important for keeping gum disease under control after treatment.

Warning Signs

When You Should Book a Gum Evaluation

🩸

Bleeding While Brushing

Healthy gums do not usually bleed regularly, so this should not be ignored.

😣

Swollen or Tender Gums

Gum inflammation may signal plaque accumulation or deeper periodontal issues.

😮

Persistent Bad Breath

Bacterial deposits below the gum line can contribute to unpleasant mouth odour.

📉

Receding Gums

If your teeth look longer than before, recession may be affecting the gum margin.

🦷

Loose Teeth

Loosening teeth can be a sign of more advanced gum and supporting bone involvement.

💬

Need Professional Assessment

Treatment Process

How Periodontal Treatment Is Planned

1

Gum Assessment

The gums are checked for bleeding, swelling, pocket depth, recession, and overall periodontal health.

2

Cause Identification

Plaque, tartar, oral hygiene issues, or deeper periodontal involvement are evaluated.

3

Cleaning or Deep Cleaning

Treatment is planned depending on the severity of buildup and how far it extends around the teeth.

4

Gum Care Guidance

You receive practical hygiene instructions and recommendations for maintaining healthier gums.

5

Maintenance Follow-Up

Periodic review helps monitor stability and reduce the risk of the condition returning or worsening.
